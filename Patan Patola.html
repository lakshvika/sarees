The Weave

The Patan Patola is a **legendary double-ikat silk saree** from Patan, Gujarat. It is famous for its vividly colored, geometrical patterns woven into the fabric by first dyeing both the warp and weft threads before weaving. The intricate, precise technique makes this one of the most time-consuming and expensive sarees in the world, often taking six months to a year to weave a single piece.

Styling Note

Best paired with (Blouse): A **simple, solid-colored silk blouse** to let the complexity and vibrancy of the double-ikat Patola pattern remain the focal point. A plain fabric in one of the saree's primary colors with a subtle matching border on the sleeves is highly recommended.
